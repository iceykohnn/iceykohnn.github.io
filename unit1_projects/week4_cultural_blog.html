One of the major strategies for identifying sources of conflict that I employ is to change my perspective. If I am arguing with someone, then my perspective is, to use a gamer vernacular, first-persone. That perspective is limited. I try to stop, calm myself and take my perspective up to third-person, meaning I'm looking down at myself and the person I'm in conflict with, from above us and over my shoulder. If I was a bystander listening to this conversation, what would I think about what I heard? About what I had heard from both people, I would try to discern what their emotions were, pride, anger, disrespect, etc. I would try to discern any motives, agendas, grudges and/or denial.

One of my greatest conflicts was with my cousin. He took my property, and gave it away, without my permission. This was highly annoying. I don't like it when people do that. So we had it out. The more I required an apology from him, the more defensive and agitated he became. Luckily there was a third person there who spoke up and broke the tension. He explained my side to my cousin, and eased the situation enough for my to gain my senses. I dropped the situation, and resigned, because I felt it was better to have peace than tension in the face of someone so unwavering. I honestly didn't want to fight anymore about it.
